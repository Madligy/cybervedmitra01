Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication (also called Multi-Factor Authentication) is an extra layer of security for your digital accounts. Instead of just requiring a password, it requires two pieces of evidence to prove you are who you say you are.

"Passwords alone are not enough. 2FA adds an extra layer of protection to your identity."

Why This Matters

If a hacker steals your password (through phishing or a data breach), 2FA prevents them from getting into your account because they won't have the second factor (like your phone or a security key).

Real-World Example

Think of it like an ATM. To get money, you need two things: Something you HAVE (your debit card) and something you KNOW (your PIN). If a thief steals your card, they still can't get money without the PIN. 2FA is the digital version of this.

Best Practices

  • Turn it ON: Enable 2FA on every account that offers it (Instagram, Google, Discord, Fortnite).
  • Use Authentication Apps: Apps like Google Authenticator or Authy are more secure than SMS (text message) codes.
  • Keep Recovery Codes Safe: When you set up 2FA, you'll get recovery codes. Save them in a safe place in case you lose your phone.
  • Never Share Codes: If someone calls and asks for your 2FA code, they are a hacker. No legitimate company will ever ask for it.
