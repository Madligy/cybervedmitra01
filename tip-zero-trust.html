Zero Trust Architecture

Zero Trust is a security concept centered on the belief that organizations should not automatically trust anything inside or outside its perimeters and instead must verify anything and everything trying to connect to its systems before granting access.

"Never trust, always verify ‚Äî every access request must be checked as if it came from an open network."

Why This Matters

In the past, security was like a castle with a moat. Once you were inside the castle, you were trusted. However, if a hacker got inside, they had access to everything. Zero Trust changes this: it assumes there are always threats, even inside the walls.

Real-World Example

Think of entering a high-security building. Instead of just showing your ID at the front door and being allowed to go anywhere, you have to show your ID and use a fingerprint scanner at every single room you want to enter. That is the essence of Zero Trust.

Best Practices

  • Continuous Verification: Always prove who you are when accessing sensitive apps.
  • Least Privilege: Only give access to the specific files or rooms someone needs, nothing more.
  • Assume Breach: Act as if a hacker is already in the system and design your safety rules around that.
  • Device Health: Ensure your phone or laptop is updated and safe before connecting to important school or work accounts.
