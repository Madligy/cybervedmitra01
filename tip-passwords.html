Strong Password Habits

Passwords are the keys to your digital life. If someone gets your password, they can access your emails, social media, and photos. Most hackers don't 'guess' passwords ‚Äî they use computers that can try millions of combinations in seconds.

"A strong password is your first line of defense. Use symbols, numbers, and mixed cases."

Why This Matters

Simple passwords like "password123" or your birthday can be cracked almost instantly. A strong, unique password makes it much harder for hackers to break into your account.

Real-World Example

Think of it like a lock on your door. A weak password is like a simple piece of tape. A strong password is like a heavy-duty deadbolt that requires a special pattern to open.

Best Practices

  • Length Matters: Use at least 12 characters. Longer is almost always stronger.
  • Mix it up: Use uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols (like @, #, $).
  • Uniqueness is Key: Never use the same password for two different accounts.
  • Use Passphrases: Instead of a word, use a sentence like "MyDogEats3Apples!@". It's easier to remember and harder to crack.
