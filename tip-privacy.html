Data Privacy Awareness

In the digital age, your personal data is like digital currency. Companies, apps, and even malicious actors are constantly looking for ways to collect it. Data privacy is about having control over who sees your information and how they use it.

"Think before sharing personal information online ‚Äî once it's out there, it's hard to take back."

Why This Matters

When you share your location, birthday, school name, or photos, you are creating a digital footprint. This information can be used to track you, target you with manipulative ads, or even commit identity theft.

Real-World Example

Imagine a fun "What Disney Character are you?" quiz on social media. Many of these quizzes require access to your profile data, including your email and friends list, just to give you a simple result. By clicking 'Accept', you might be handing over your private data to an unknown developer.

Best Practices

  • Check Privacy Settings: Regularly review settings on Instagram, Snapchat, and other apps. Set your profile to 'Private'.
  • Minimize Sharing: Don't share your phone number, home address, or current location in public posts.
  • Read App Permissions: Ask yourself: "Does this game really need access to my camera and contacts?"
  • Audit Your Footprint: Google yourself occasionally to see what information is public.
