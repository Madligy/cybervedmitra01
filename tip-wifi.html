Public Wi-Fi Safety

Public Wi-Fi in cafes, airports, and malls is convenient, but it's often insecure. Anyone else on the same network could potentially 'sniff' or see the data you are sending and receiving if it's not encrypted.

"Free Wi-Fi can expose your data. Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on open networks."

Why This Matters

Hackers can set up fake Wi-Fi hotspots with names like "Free Mall Wi-Fi." If you connect to them, they can see exactly what you do online, including the passwords you type or the messages you send.

Real-World Example

Imagine you're at the airport and connect to "Airport_Free_Wifi." You decide to check your bank account or log into your school portal. A person sitting three seats away could be using a simple tool to intercept that connection and see your login details in real-time.

Best Practices

  • Avoid Sensitive Tasks: Never log into social media, banks, or email on public Wi-Fi. Use it only for general browsing (like reading news).
  • Use Your Mobile Data: If you need to log in to something, turn off Wi-Fi and use your phone's 4G/5G instead. It's much safer.
  • Forget the Network: Once you're done, tell your device to "Forget this network" so it doesn't reconnect automatically later.
  • Don't Be Lured: If you see two networks with similar names and one doesn't require a password, be very suspicious.
