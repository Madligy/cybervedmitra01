Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where hackers pretend to be someone you trust ‚Äî like your school, a bank, or a popular brand like Amazon ‚Äî to trick you into giving away your passwords or clicking on malicious links.

"Scammers use urgency to trick users into clicking. Stop, look at the sender, and think."

Why This Matters

Phishing is one of the most common ways people get hacked. It's not about tech skill; it's about tricking your brain into making a mistake because you're rushed or scared.

Real-World Example

You receive an email that looks exactly like Instagram, saying: "Unusual login detected! Click here to secure your account immediately or it will be deleted in 1 hour." The urgency makes you want to click fast, but the link actually goes to a fake login page that steals your password.

Best Practices

  • Check the Sender: Look closely at the email address. Is it "support@instagram.com" or "support@insta-secure-login.net"?
  • Go to the Source: Never click links in suspicious emails. Instead, go to the official website or app yourself.
  • Beware of Urgency: If an email says you must act "right now" or "within 5 minutes," it's probably a scam.
  • Hover Before Clicking: Hover your mouse over a link to see where it actually leads before you click it.
